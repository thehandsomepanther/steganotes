Steganotes

Over-air data transfer via audio signals.

This project takes inspiration from Google Tone and dial-up Internet, both of which use audio tones to transfer data (in the case of Google Tone, it's to share a URL).

Steganotes goes one step further and investigates if we can transfer data of arbitrary length over-the-air using those same underlying principles.

How it Works

To encode data in an audio signal, you just have to provide the data you'd like to transfer. Steganotes will compute the spectrogram of the signal (by default, a sinewave) and step through it in the time domain, maxing out the frequency bin specified by the decimal value of the byte.
