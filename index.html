Motivation

This project takes inspiration from Google Tone and dial-up Internet, both of which use audio tones to transfer data (in the case of Google Tone, it's to share a URL).

Steganotes goes one step further and investigates if we can transfer text of arbitrary length over-the-air using those same underlying principles.

How it Works

To encode data in an audio signal, you just have to provide the data you'd like to transfer. Steganotes will compute the spectrogram of the signal (by default, a sinewave) and step through it in the time domain, maxing out the frequency bin specified by the decimal value of the byte.

// this is not the actual code, but it gives you a good idea of what's going on
t = 0
for byte in datafile:
  bin = toDecimal(byte)
  spec[bin][t] = a number higher than the max value in spec[:][t]
  t += 1

Results

We tested the system by encoding data into a wav file and playing it through a pair of laptop speakers while another speaker listened for the signal and decoded once it was received. Accuracy of the decoding varied based on distance, volume, and background noise.
